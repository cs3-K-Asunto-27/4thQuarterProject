Environmental Impact

Genetic modification can lead to unintended environmental consequences, such as the disruption of ecosystems and the loss of biodiversity.

Health Concerns

Some genetically modified organisms (GMOs) may pose risks to human health, such as allergic reactions or the development of antibiotic resistance.

Ethical Dilemmas

The use of genetic modification raises ethical questions about the manipulation of living organisms and the potential consequences for future generations.

Unintended Consequences

Genetic modification can have unpredictable outcomes, leading to unintended consequences that may only become apparent over time.

Regulation and Oversight

The regulation and oversight of genetic modification technologies may be insufficient to address potential risks adequately.
